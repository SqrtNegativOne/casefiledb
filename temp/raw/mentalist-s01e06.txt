## Recap
The California Bureau of Investigation is gathered on the highway at the border between California and Nevada to investigate a severed hand with a pinky ring and the number 43 written on it. Patrick Jane examines the hand and draws some surprising conclusions regarding the victim. Cho doubts the veracity of his deductions. Patrick bets Cho that he is correct. Lisbon joins the group and verifies Patrick's assertion that the victim, Jim Meier , runs a large casino and spa in Nevada. As always, he has remarkable insight into the people and situations around him. He wins the bet. Because the hand is inside the California border they have caught the case.
Lisbon and Van Pelt inform the next of kin, Meier's wife, Ann Meier , their daughter Jessica , and Jessica's husband Daniel , of the grisly news of their loved one's demise. While they question the family, Jane wanders around using his incredible observational abilities to piece together a profile of the victim and his family, in order to determine possible suspects for the crime. Patrick talks briefly with Jessica in the other room about her relationship with her father and finds out that he wasn't around a lot as she was growing up. Jessica returns to the group as Lisbon continues her questioning; they all notice Daniel's inappropriate response to a football game during this rather sensitive discussion. The team tries to determine if robbery was a motive for the murder and finds out that Meier wore a gold casino chip worth $100,000 around his neck.
Rigsby calls from the office to discuss his research on similar murders in the past and has found that there was a mob connection to those murders. They discuss the possibility that Meier was involved with organized crime. Lisbon, Van Pelt and Jane proceed to the casino to speak to Matt Etienne , the head of security. While Lisbon and Van Pelt look over some investigative material and speak to Etienne regarding the case, Patrick moves through the casino, gambling and observing. He uses his mental acuity to play and begins to win big, but there is obviously a method to his success. The casino realizes he is a card counter and flag him as a cheater. Not so coincidentally, Patrick has chosen table 43 to work his magic. Etienne approaches Patrick and asks him to leave the table. As he leaves the table with Matt, Patrick quietly exposes the dealer, Alexandra Yee , and her accomplice as cheaters.
Back at the office, Patrick joins Lisbon and listens to the interrogation of the Alexandra. She was using the money she stole to pay for her mother's liver transplant. Cho accuses her of killing Meier because he had discovered her thievery. Of course she denies it. Patrick wonders why Meier didn't inform Etienne of the theft. They postulate that Etienne was perhaps in on the scam. Patrick interrupts the interrogation to ask the Alexandra if Meier had asked her if Etienne was involved. She had told Meier that he was not. Jane asks her if Etienne was indeed involved but Alexandra denies it. Patrick obviously believes her and to Lisbon's chagrin, he tells Alexandra that she can leave. Patrick explains that by letting her go, they will be able to set up the real killer by making him suspicious of what the Alexandra had told the police.
Patrick then presents Lisbon, Van Pelt, Rigsby and Cho with some extravagant jewelry and watches that he purchased with his $250K winnings. The group head out for a relaxing dinner celebration; however, after their dinner, the women return their jewelry, though the men decide to keep the gifts. Later, we see Jane put the jewelry and most of his winnings in a charity bin. He keeps a small stake for his next gambling session.
The next day, in the Meier's home, Teresa, Grace and Patrick meet with Ann, Jessica and Daniel. Ann confesses that she was having an affair with Etienne, but vehemently denies that she murdered her husband. During the discussion, Patrick observes a strain in the relationship between Jessica and Daniel. He quietly approaches Daniel and asks him to set him up with in a high-stakes poker game. The con begins.
Teresa and Patrick go to Etienne's house to execute a search. When Jane notices some suspicious footprints outside the garage, they begin their search. Meier's body is found in the freezer. The interrogation of Etienne yields little. It appears that he knows nothing about the murder. Etienne explains that he had met with Ann at their motel.
The autopsy report reveals that Meier died of blunt force trauma to the head and has abrasions around his neck, where they speculate that his lucky chip was ripped off his neck. They begin to look at the recordings Meier used to spy on the casino guests to see if anything of use is revealed.
Patrick enters the high-stakes game with Cal Trask , one of the big spenders at the casino. Daniel also joins. Back at the Meier's home, Rigsby and Cho search for the surveillance tape. When they find the DVD, they discover that Meiers had recorded Jessica meeting with Trask in his hotel room. When Trask is questioned, he reveals Jessica's husband used her as collateral for one of his gambling debts.
In the poker game, Patrick is doing very well but so is Daniel. They begin a head to head game. Patrick begins his trap. He uses a sleight of hand to set up Daniel with quad kings (four of a kind) and "accidentally" shows Daniel his hand of trip sevens, to entice Daniel to bet everything he has. Daniel pulls out the missing $100k chip and plays it. To Daniel's astonishment, Patrick lays down a straight flush, beating him. As he leaves, Rigsby and Cho arrest him. Daniel confesses.
Patrick returns Meier's lucky chip to his family and tells Jessica that her father died protecting her.
A nurse brings a case to Alexandra, who is sitting by her mother's hospital bed. The nurse explains that a really cute blond guy brought the case by for her. When Alexandra opens it, we discover what Patrick had spent his $300K on. She looks at the money with tears of joy in her eyes.

## Case Resolution (SPOILER)
Jim Meier was murdered by his son-in-law Daniel Cardeira . Deep in gambling debts, Daniel used his wife Jessica Meier as collateral with high roller Cal Trask , which Meier discovered through his secret surveillance recordings. When Meier confronted him, Daniel killed him with blunt force trauma, stole his lucky $100,000 casino chip, and tried to cover it up by hiding the body in Matt Etienne’s garage freezer. Jane exposed Daniel during a high-stakes poker game by baiting him into betting the stolen chip, leading to his arrest and confession.

## Trivia
This is one of the only episodes in the series whose investigation takes place outside the state of California (in this case, the entire investigation takes place between the Nevada-California border and the fictional city of Calida, Nevada, where the victim of the episode, Jim Meier , lived with his family and owned Calida Hotel & Resort).

## Goofs
If Daniel had put two cards back in his poker game with Patrick instead of just one, Patrick would not have been able to complete his straight-flush and would have lost to Daniel's three-of-a-kind (or less probable a full house). This might have prevented Daniel's confession and Patrick having $300,000 to give Alexandra for her sick mother's surgery. As putting back two cards would have been a valid line of play since three-of-a-kind is more valuable than a Full house, Patrick having seemingly left so much up to chance was likely an oversight on the part of The Mentalist's writing staff. The above should be edited or removed. While trading two cards in would be the correct play based on Daniel's cards (though not for the reasons stated above), Patrick only gained $5k off the last hand (he gave the $100k chip to the Meiers). He already had the $300k to give away, and would have just folded without the straight flush. Patrick was clearly certain Daniel was guilty, and he could have gained the chip any number of ways. It's more likely this was meant to show Daniel's weaknesses as a cards player that had been frequently alluded to and to show Patrick reading him and entertaining himself, rather than an oversight. In addition, Patrick starts with 7, 8, and 9 of hearts in his hand and ends with a straight to the jack. It's likely the next three cards in the deck after the king were (in order): J of hearts, 10 of hearts, and 6 of hearts so even if Daniel had asked for two cards, Patrick would still have a straight flush but to the ten instead. Though this would rely on Daniel not being too observant.
