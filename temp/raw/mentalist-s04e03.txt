## Summary
Jane and the CBI team are assigned with a kidnapping case, the victim being the son of a former client from Jane's days as a psychic. Meanwhile, Jane encounters his former client's new "spiritual advisor".

## Recap
This episode starts with a missing child, Conner Flint. The boy's mother, Elizabeth (or "Beth" for short), was one of Jane ’s clients back in the day and still believes he’s psychic. Furthermore, she has a new psychic that’s obviously a fraud. No one is more desperate for hope than Elizabeth. She just refuses to believe Jane's confession, as Elizabeth keeps berating him for denying his gift and not using it to find her child. Enter her latest “spiritual adviser”, Nate Glass , who’s conveniently been around ever since Elizabeth's wealthy husband James passed away two years prior from cancer.
Upon investigation into Conner’s kidnapping at his bus stop, the team discovers that two other boys had been taken the same way and had been found dead within 12 hours by someone the media has dubbed “ The Balloon Man ”, because he uses the balloons to lure his victims away with him. Looks like the clock is ticking on poor Conner. Lisbon and Jane are still at the house and talk to Conner’s older half-brother, Jonathan , and his Uncle Deke. Seems Jonathan owns everything and just lets his step-mother and Conner live there free. It’s also obvious that neither Jonathan nor Deke like Jane for fleecing Beth all those years ago. Jane snaps right back at them with the “tells” of their addictions (gambling for Deke, antidepressants for Jonathan). Bottom line out of these two is that it was a typical morning. They had breakfast, then Conner went to the school bus stop. He waited there alone because he didn’t want to be teased for needing someone to wait with him.
Back at the office, they’ve hauled in the “usual suspects” for interviews and they don’t seem very productive, except mention of a ubiquitous black SUV. Jane’s pretty quiet, except for his efforts to convince Beth that Glass is a fraud, even going so far as to tell Glass that he would've let him run his game if a kid’s life wasn’t at stake. The team keeps hunting the Balloon Man and discovers that the vantage point the kidnapper would have needed to know the kids were at the bus stops alone would be a telephone pole – leading back to the very same suspect that pointed them towards the SUV. Jane stays behind “to think” while the rest of the team go to bring him in. Van Pelt sneaks in from behind and takes him out. Jane’s left them a note in Rigsby ’s pocket – the Balloon Man didn’t take Conner. The whole thing does give Van Pelt a chance to mess with Rigsby too, by telling him that she thinks she’s starting to enjoy shooting people.
Jane’s still not sure who did take Conner, although he suspects it’s something more “tangled”. Further research shows that Beth is in a lot of debt and Conner was left with half of the inheritance when her husband died, not her. Beth only gets the money, which is kept in a trust account until Conner turns 30, if her son dies. So the team brings her in for questioning. Beth is very believable when she says her life would be over if anything happened to Conner. Jane is thinking “love is strange” and that he’d like a sandwich.
Jane’s ready to set up the suspects to see who bites, but this time he’s doing it in style, with an award-worthy performance of a “reading” of Conner. He uses all the clues he’s gathered so far to push the kidnapper into a corner where he has to do something. If he doesn’t, the team will find Connor--that’s exactly what Jane wants and the team is actually poised to follow the kidnapper to wherever he has Connor. It was Jonathan, who still hadn’t decided if he could kill his brother, though he insisted he was going to bring Connor back. Either way though, by comforting his stepmother after Connor was found dead, or being the hero that rescued him, Jonathan thought he could spin it to win Beth’s heart.
Jane and Lisbon take Conner home, where they are greeted by Beth, who is overjoyed to see her son come home alive. After Beth, Deke, Lisbon, and Conner get inside the house, Glass comes up to Jane and confronts him about stealing his act. Jane proceeds to tell Glass that his con man act is "weak" but Glass replies by mimicking his psychic act. He doesn't know that Jane is recording their conversation the whole time.
Beth asks Jane how he knew and Jane explains how he suspects and observes. She talks about his psychic life and Jane apologizes, but Beth assures him that even though Jane did deceive her, she is still thankful that he gave her hope.
