## Intro
"You Can't Handle This Episode" is the tenth episode of Season Four . Shawn tangles with the Army when he claims that a private's suicide was actually murder, and suspects Juliet 's former military/current mercenary brother is involved.

## Plot Summary
In 1989, Henry gets on Shawn for his watching television at noon while still in his pajamas. Shawn is enamored with a commercial for the U.S. Army, much the satisfaction of his father — until Shawn proclaims that he's more interested in the jingle the commercial used.
In the present, Lassiter and Juliet are at the scene of a possible suicide with Gus , Shawn and Abigail . It appears the victim hanged himself, but Shawn sees signs of murder and discovers dog tags stuffed down the dead man's throat. This is not just a murder now, it's a military crime, which poses jurisdictional problems. But, Juliet says she knows someone who might be able to help with that, and she calls her brother Ewan , an ex-military man who is now hired out by the government for covert missions. He is in the middle of finishing up a job, but he is able to tell Juliet that swallowing dog tags is a sign of shame. He then tells her he's near Santa Barbara and is coming in for a visit.
The next day, Ewan is at the police station meeting the gang when the autopsy report comes in, supporting the murder theory. They will now need to go to the base and ask questions. Juliet thinks Ewan could be of assistance and asks him to come along. At the gate to the base, he flashes his credentials and they are immediately admitted. There, they meet Major General Felts, who tells them that Starks' death is a military matter. But, Lassiter and Juliet insist on seeing Stark's belongings and tracing his movements that day. Felts acquiesces and tells his assistant Lt. Wallach to take them to Starks' barracks and to the motor pool where he worked. The group decides to split up, and Ewan goes with Shawn and Gus to the motor pool where Shawn discovers that Starks signed out of work on Monday. A private there tells them it could be because he was either sick, injured, or had a meeting with a superior. Just then, Ewan gets a call from his boss, who knows where he is and what he's doing. He tells Ewan to root out the bad seed on the base and cover it up. When Ewan hangs up, another private hands him a file on Starks. Ewan rips out the page where it says Starks was meeting with Felts on Monday and shreds it.
The next day at the police station, Lassiter and Juliet say they found nothing in their search through Starks' belongings. Ewan says he's sorry the file didn't turn up anything more, either. He then says he thinks it might have been a suicide all along and unfortunately can't get them back on the base again. Shawn looks at the file and notices a page has been torn out. He wonders at Ewan's sudden lack of desire to help and suspects Ewan might somehow be involved. Shawn and Gus decide to sneak back onto the base themselves, but are caught and brought to Felts. Felts warns them not to try that again. As the guys leave, they see Ewan talking to a soldier. His back is to them, so he doesn't notice they are there.
At the police station the next morning, Shawn and Gus let Lassiter in on their suspicions of Ewan. Juliet overhears and is irate. Felts comes to the station as a courtesy, but shuts down when Lassiter turns the conversation into an interrogation. Shawn suggests to Juliet that they get Ewan to help them get back on the base, that way they can tell which side he's really on. Ewan, then, takes Shawn, Gus and Juliet to the weapons depot at the base. There, they learn that Starks sometimes substituted for Templeton when Templeton wanted to sneak off to meet his girlfriend. Starks must have seen something suspicious while working there. Ewan gets another call from his boss. Ewan tells him that Shawn is too good to slow down, so he will now let the psychic lead him to the target and take care of it from there.
That night, Shawn, Ewan and Gus stake out the weapons depot and sneak onto the back of a truck as it pulls out. The truck stops at a remote air strip where they catch Wallach selling weapons on the side. This is what Starks must have stumbled across when he filled in for Templeton. He was going to tell Felts, but Wallach killed him to cover his tracks. Wallach spots them and takes off. The buyers shower the guys with a hail of bullets, but Ewan is able to get them all out alive.
Back at the base, Felts says he will track the stolen weapons and set up a manhunt for Wallach. Ewan tells them he's got another assignment and is unable to help with the search. He leaves but Shawn remembers that he had been working against them for a while. Suspicious, Shawn and Gus follow Ewan to a pier where he has Wallach tied up, with a gun trained on him. Ewan is about to complete his mission and clean up the situation. He tells Shawn and Gus to leave for their own good but Juliet and Lassiter arrive and are forced to arrest Ewan. Later, as Juliet and Lassiter wait to meet Ewan as he is transferred to a different prison, they learn he is mysteriously no longer in custody. Meanwhile, Shawn and Gus are at the airport, saying goodbye to Abigail who is moving to Africa to teach, leaving her relationship with Shawn up in the air.

## Trivia
The title is inspired by Jack Nicholson's famous line from the 1992 court-martial drama A Few Good Men : "You can't handle the truth!" It also fits with John Cena 's signature wrestling "You Can't See Me" handwave taunt. John Cena is one of several wrestlers to make an appearance in the Psychverse, and the episode also contains a reference to Kamala (James Arthur Harris). Cena wrestles for the WWE, whose shows are also broadcast by USA Network , and in return for Cena's appearance on Psych , James Roday Rodriguez and Dulé Hill got to guest-host Monday Night Raw on January 25, 2010 (although James could only phone in as he was hospitalized at the time). There's a mug decorated with a pineapple on the refreshments table at the police station, Abigail has a ceramic pineapple jar in her bedroom (with the lid detached), and Felts has an Mk 2 grenade (commonly nicknamed "Pineapple") on his desk. Several of the soldiers involved in this episode were named after members of the Psych crew: Franks, Tuke, Lejsek, Holbrook, Berman and Lukacevic (whose fatigues Shawn wears) were named after Steve Franks (the show's creator), Simon Tuke (especially appropriate since he designed the costumes for this episode and many others), James Lejsek ( Chris Henze 's assistant), Trevor Holbrook (camera assistant), Andy Berman (writer and producer) and Melissa Lukacevic (assistant director). Shawn tells Franks he's the Paraguayan ambassador, which is the same office he claimed to hold in S1E12 " Cloudy... With a Chance of Murder ". Gus brings up the fact that Lassiter and Juliet arrested both of his parents at Christmas, which happened in S2E10 " Gus' Dad May Have Killed an Old Guy ". Shawn calls Franks "Gomer", which is a reference to Vincent D'Onofrio's character Gomer Pyle in the 1987 war drama Full Metal Jacket . There's a reference to the communal beating Gomer receives in that film when Gus is afraid Felts will "come back with a bar of soap wrapped in a towel" and Shawn replies "There's no way we're getting D'Onofrioed." Shawn's joke about pitching a show called Army Psychic to CBS is a little jibe at CBS's The Mentalist . Steve Franks placed several Mentalist jokes throughout the show to draw attention to the fact that it basically stole the entire premise he came up with. Shawn tells Felts he has no respect for the Coast Guard and asks him whether he's seen The Guardian without hiding his distaste for it. Gus's annoyed look not only indicates he doesn't want Shawn to antagonize Felts any further, but is also a jokey reminder that Dulé Hill had a small role in that film.
