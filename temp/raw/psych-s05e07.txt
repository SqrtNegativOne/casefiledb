## Intro
"Ferry Tale" is the seventh episode of Season Five . Shawn and Gus help a prison guard locate his escaped inmates while on a ferry boat headed to an eco-project in the Channel Islands.

## Plot Summary
In 1990, Shawn and Gus have their legs duct taped together in preparation for the three-legged race at the police department picnic, where the winner gets a new bike. The kids set a new personal best in their time trials, but the jubilation soon turns to arguing when they can't decide who will get to keep the new bike if they win it.
In the present day, Shawn and Gus board a ferry bound for the Channel Islands . They're volunteering (though Shawn doesn't know it at first) for environmental clean-up, along with other nature lovers and a group of four prisoners on work detail. After Gus gets seasick, he and Shawn take a wrong turn and run into Craig Snowden , the prison guard, who has been tied up and gagged. Craig tells them that the prisoners he was guarding escaped, are loose on the ferry, and are armed with his gun. Shawn calls Henry and tells him the news. Henry, in turn, tells Shawn to stay put and not take matters into his own hands. Shawn, of course, does the opposite, as he and Gus ambush the prisoners and ruin their means of escape by dropping a life raft into the water. The prisoners' next course of action is to take everyone hostage.
Dane Northcutt, one of the prisoners, speaks with Chief Vick and gives his list of demands. He then ups the stakes - if the demands are not met in a reasonable time and they are unable to safely escape the ferry, then they will start shooting hostages. The SBPD surrounds the ferry and assesses the massive hostage situation they now have on their hands. Shawn is able to get himself isolated with the prisoners, informing them that the police will storm the ferry if they feel the hostages aren't safe. The prisoners respond by getting up on deck and having the hostages form a circle around them to protect them. A tense countdown ensues, and just as we think Northcutt has carried out his threat and shot two innocent hostages, tear gas is launched onto the boat. SBPD storms the ferry, apprehends the inmates, and rushes the injured hostages to safety. But, Shawn then swiftly points out that two of the injured "hostages" they took were actually inmates in disguise. So, now, they have two prisoners on the loose, one of which is Northcutt.
Back at SBPD, Craig has been fired, and Shawn believes there was a third accomplice in addition to the two escaped prisoners. Shawn remembers a tattoo on a few of the inmates - Tres Diablos - which they learn is a gang run by Northcutt's former cellmate, Percy Dunn - but Craig tells them that Percy has actually been dead for two years. They then realize it wasn't Percy they need to talk to, but rather Percy's mother - Lorraine Dunn. Lorraine just happens to be a GED tutor at the prison, and Northcutt has been one of her regular students for the past two years.
Shawn, Gus, and Craig pay her a visit in her home. Lorraine shows them a photo of her with her son when he was little. The picture was taken in front of a church, and she explains that Percy used to help her clean the church as a way of staying out of trouble. However, when the church was boarded up, Percy started running with gangs. She says she hasn't seen Northcutt, but Shawn and Craig see through the lie, eventually getting her to admit that Northcutt stopped by and visited Percy's room to pay his respects. Shawn searches the room and discovers that Northcutt had taken a hidden key from the vent. They investigate further and discover that Percy was serving prison time for an armed robbery, and there were rumors that $1 million was never recovered. Percy hid the money and, knowing that he wasn't going to live out his sentence, gave Northcutt the location for him to find once he broke out of prison (which was what the key was for). Shawn then gets a clue to go visit Northcutt's girlfriend and son.
During his visit, Shawn figures out the money was supposed to go to Northcutt's son, and that Northcutt had been trying to find the church. Shawn then realizes that's where the money is hidden. At the church, the team looks for the money. The team finds Northcutt there with the money and is thankfully able to take him, along with his accomplice, down. But, in a stunning turn of events, Craig points a gun on Shawn, Gus, and the two inmates. Craig, distraught from losing his job, wants the money for himself. So, he steals it from the inmates and makes an escape in The Blueberry . He is stopped shortly by Shawn and Gus. They plead with Craig not to take the money and try to appeal to him on his level. Craig points the gun at Shawn and Gus and shoots - but Shawn and Gus aren't hit. Craig ends up shooting and injuring an approaching Northcutt and his partner, Sanfus Sanders.
Back at SBPD, Shawn and Gus lie on Craig's behalf in order to get his job back. And as a result, Craig is given a $100,000 reward for returning the stolen money. Shawn and Gus fully expect to get a little monetary pat on the back for not telling on Craig to Chief Vick, but Craig decides to keep the money for himself as he heads off into the sunset.

## Trivia
A variation on the more standard 'fairy tale', given the marine setting, is used for the title. Shawn makes a reference to Percy Jackson and the Olympians—mistakenly calling Percy Dunn “The Lightning Thief.” There is a Psych reference in a Percy Jackson series novel, The Demigod Diaries . A pineapple can be spotted on the tote bag of one of the ferry passengers. This episode marks the first appearance of Skyler Gisondo as Young Shawn.
